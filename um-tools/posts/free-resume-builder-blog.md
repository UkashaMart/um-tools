## Resume Writing Tips

### Keep Your Summary Short
Two to three sentences highlighting your strengths is enough. Recruiters skim resumes quickly.

### Use Action Words
Words like "managed", "built", "led", and "improved" make your experience sound more impactful than passive descriptions.

### List Recent Experience First
Always put your most recent job at the top of your experience section.

### Match Skills to the Job
Only list skills that are relevant to the position you're applying for.

### One Page is Often Best
Especially for students and early-career professionals, a single page resume is usually preferred.
