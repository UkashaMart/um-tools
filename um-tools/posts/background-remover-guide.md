## How Background Removal Actually Works

There are two broad approaches to removing a background from an image, and understanding the difference helps you pick the right tool and get better results.

**AI-based segmentation** uses a trained neural network to recognize the shape of a person, animal, or object in a photo and separate it from everything else, regardless of what is behind it. This is powerful because it can handle busy, complex backgrounds like a street scene or a living room. The tradeoff is that this approach typically requires either a paid cloud service or a large AI model that needs to be downloaded and run on your device, which can be slow and inconsistent depending on your browser and hardware.

**Color-based removal**, also known as chroma keying, takes a simpler and more reliable approach. Instead of trying to recognize objects, it looks at the color of the background and removes any pixel that closely matches that color, while keeping pixels that differ from it. This is exactly the technique used in film and television for green screen effects, just applied automatically to photos instead of video.

Our free background remover uses this second approach. It automatically samples the color in the corners of your uploaded image, assumes that is your background color, and removes every pixel that closely matches it. The result is processed entirely inside your browser using the Canvas API built into every modern browser, which means your photo is never uploaded to any server.

## Tips for Getting the Cleanest Results

A few small adjustments before you even take the photo can make background removal far more effective:

- **Use a genuinely solid background.** Wrinkled fabric, shadows, or gradient backdrops introduce color variation that can confuse a simple color match. A flat, evenly lit backdrop works best.
- **Increase contrast between subject and background.** If your subject is white and is sitting on a white background, parts of it may get removed along with the background. Choose a backdrop color that contrasts with your subject.
- **Light the scene evenly.** Shadows on the backdrop create darker shades of the same color, which can be handled with the Edge Smoothness setting, but starting with even lighting reduces how much correction is needed.
- **Start with a higher resolution photo.** Higher resolution images give the tool more detail to work with, which generally produces cleaner edges around fine details like hair or fabric texture.

## Common Questions

**Will this work on a photo with a busy or outdoor background?**
Not well. Since this tool targets a specific background color rather than recognizing objects, backgrounds with multiple colors and complex scenes, like a street or a forest, will not separate cleanly. This tool is built specifically for solid or near-solid backgrounds.

**Is my photo uploaded to a server?**
No. Every part of the process, from detecting the background color to making pixels transparent, happens directly in your browser using your device's own processing power. Your image never leaves your computer or phone.

**What if the result cuts off part of my subject?**
This usually means the sensitivity is set too high, causing it to treat parts of your subject as background. Lower the sensitivity slider slightly and the cutout will become more conservative.
