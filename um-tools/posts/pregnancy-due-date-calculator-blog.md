# Pregnancy Due Date Calculator — Know Your Estimated Due Date

Congratulations on your pregnancy! One of the first questions every expecting parent asks is: when is my baby due? Our free due date calculator gives you an instant estimate.

## How Due Dates Are Calculated

### Naegele's Rule (Last Menstrual Period Method)
The most common method estimates your due date as 280 days (40 weeks) from the first day of your last menstrual period, assuming a standard 28-day cycle. If your cycle is shorter or longer, the calculation adjusts accordingly.

### Conception Date Method
If you know your conception date, your due date is estimated at 266 days (38 weeks) from that date.

### IVF Transfer Date Method
For IVF pregnancies, due date is calculated based on your embryo transfer date, accounting for the embryo's age at transfer.

## Understanding Pregnancy Trimesters

| Trimester | Weeks | What Happens |
|-----------|-------|--------------|
| First | 1-13 | Early development, morning sickness is common |
| Second | 14-27 | Often called the "golden period" — energy typically returns |
| Third | 28-40+ | Final growth phase, body prepares for birth |

## How Accurate Is a Due Date Calculator?

It's important to set realistic expectations: only about 5% of babies are born exactly on their calculated due date. Most babies arrive within two weeks before or after the estimated date. Your due date is a helpful planning tool, not a guarantee.

## Gestational Age vs Fetal Age

Doctors typically use "gestational age," counted from your last menstrual period. This is different from "fetal age," which is counted from conception — gestational age is usually about two weeks ahead of fetal age.

## When to See a Doctor

Always confirm your due date with a healthcare provider, ideally through an early ultrasound, which tends to be more accurate than calculation methods alone, especially if your cycles are irregular.
